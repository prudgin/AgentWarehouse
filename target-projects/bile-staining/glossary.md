# Glossary — 2025 Bile Staining

Domain vocabulary for the RAS-quarantine bile-staining trial. Add new terms here when they first resolve (in conversation, in an INVESTIGATION, or while authoring a report). Each entry: term in **bold**, then a short definition. Link the term back to the source that resolved it where possible.

## Trial design

**Static tank** — RAS-quarantine tank (~1 m³) used in this trial with no recirculation or flow-through; oxygen delivered via diffusion stones. Top-ups and partial exchanges are allowed but not continuous flow. The experimental unit. Proposal: `Proposal/Bile staining proposal 29-10-2025.docx`.

**Purge** — withholding feed (nil-by-mouth) for a fixed period before harvest. Trial purge durations: 2, 4, 6, 8 days. Last-feed time is recorded exactly per group.

**Run** — one filling of the 8-tank system: 4 tanks on the short purge, 4 on the long purge. Stage 1 uses two consecutive runs (Run A: 2 vs 4 days; Run B: 6 vs 8 days). Stage 2 repeats both pairings with copper status layered in.

**Stage** — a complete two-run sweep at one fish-size class. Stage 1 ≈ 1.0 kg fish; Stage 2 ≈ 1.5–2.5 kg fish.

**Good / poor condition** — operational fish-condition categories used for allocation. "Good" = well-conditioned, sourced from high-performing ponds; "poor" = lean/runty, sourced from low-performing ponds. No predefined morphometric thresholds — selection is by pond performance plus visual judgement.

**Copper factor** — whether the fish lot's source pond is on copper or had copper withdrawn ≤ 5 days pre-harvest. Recorded per fish lot. Formal in Stage 2.

## Primary outcomes (per-fish scoring)

**Bile staining severity** — visual 0–4 scale of staining in the belly cavity. 0 = none; 1 = slight (< 1 cm², one side); 2 = pronounced (2–3 cm², or both sides); 3 = extensive (3–7 cm²); 4 = covers > 7 cm² and both sides. Defined in the proposal.

**Gallbladder length** — measured in mm with a ruler at slaughter.

**Bile colour** — visual 0–4 scale: 0 = clear/very light yellow; 1 = light straw; 2 = dark yellow; 3 = yellow-green; 4 = dark green.
