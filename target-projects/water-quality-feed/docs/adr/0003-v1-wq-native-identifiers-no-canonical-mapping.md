# v1 publishes WQ-native identifiers; canonical Mercatus mapping deferred

The natural expectation for a pipeline whose stated purpose is "MDF-compatible output" is a `MercatusUnitId` column on `readings.parquet`. v1 does not have one. Reason: the WQ → Mercatus mapping is not a 1:1 lookup. WQ farms fall into two patterns (see [docs/domain/unit-mapping-puzzle.md](../domain/unit-mapping-puzzle.md)): pond-as-unit farms where one WQ unit corresponds 1:1 to one Mercatus unit, and cages-in-pond farms where one WQ site-level reading must fan out to every Mercatus child unit under the pond. The fan-out depends on MDF's `units.parquet` hierarchy and requires an investigation pass to (a) classify each farm by pattern, (b) choose the Pattern-1 mapping registry (extend `WQ_Units` SP list, or maintain a separate file, or fuzzy-match on names), and (c) confirm MDF's hierarchy exposes the parent-pond relationship Pattern 2 needs. Blocking v1 on this investigation would block the mirror — and the mirror has independent value (the WQ list is currently SharePoint-only; getting it into parquet form unlocks ad-hoc analysis even without the canonical join). So v1 ships the mirror with WQ-native identifiers only, and columns are deliberately prefixed `WQFarm/WQSite/WQUnit/WQUnitId` so no consumer can accidentally join on the wrong key. The canonical-mapping stage is tracked as the lead entry in `docs/planning/future-work.md` and will add a `MercatusUnitId` column (with fan-out rows for Pattern 2 readings) when it ships. The cost: every current consumer either treats WQ as standalone or codes the mapping themselves until the stage ships. Acceptable for v1 — the alternative is no mirror at all.
